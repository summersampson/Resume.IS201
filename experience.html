Work Experience

  1. Animal Shelter Technician - North Utah Valley Animal Shelter, Lindon
    • May 2023 - August 2023
    • My main jobs were to help people that were looking for their animals, dropping off donations, or looking to adopt new animals. I also worked directly with the animals, cleaning and caring from them. I was also able to do behavioral checks on animals that were going out for adoption, as well as vaccinate and microchip scan the animals. Because the animals were often feral, this experience taught me how to work calmly in a high-pressure environment, and how to help people in distress.
  2. Jewelry Sales Associate/Welder - Eterna, Orem
    • September 2022 - May 2023
    • Eterna is a permanent jewelry business, meaning that I welded jewelry directly around the customer. This job taught me a lot about welding, as well as how to work with people and make sure that customers are safe and happy with their products. Because I started this job when it was opening for the first time, I was able to help to shape the business into a fully functional operation.
  3. Silversmith/Jewelry Workshop Teacher - Valore, Provo
    • May 2022 - August 2022
    • At Valore, I made jewelry with gems and metal. I also taught classes of 2-8 people, helping them make their own jewelry. Leading the classes took 1.5-6 hours and demanded that I be focused and able to teach people simply and clearly. This was a super good job for me because I loved being able to work with others and get to know the customers. It was super important that everything was done perfectly because if their piece got messed up then they could have lost hundreds of dollars and I would need to redo the project.
  4. Independent Cleaner - Airbnb Independent Cleaner, American Fork
    • October 2021 - March 2022
    • For this job, I cleaned an Airbnb multiple times a week. It was a 9-room house with 5 beds. I had to work quickly and efficiently because often one group would check out and that same night a new group would come in. Being an independent worker taught me how to manage myself, stay productive, and track my own work.
  5. Shift Lead/Mixologist - Sodalicous, Orem
    • March 2021 - April 2022
    • Sodalicious was my first experience to be a Shift Lead, as I was promoted after two months. My tasks included taking orders, preparing drinks, cookies, and popcorn, and cleaning. I loved being able to develop sales skills, such as upselling the products. Through being a Shift Lead, I learned how to manage others in a successful way.
  6. Team Member - YogurtLand, Orem

I learned a lot about the importance of being confident and professional while working with people. At Yogurtland I also prepared food and cleaned the store.
